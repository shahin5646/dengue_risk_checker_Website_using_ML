Dengue Prevention & Recovery Tips

Follow these expert-backed guidelines to reduce your dengue risk and support a smooth recovery process.

Use Mosquito Repellents
Apply repellents with DEET, picaridin, or IR3535 on exposed skin and clothing, especially during early morning and late afternoon when mosquitoes are most active.

Wear Protective Clothing
Light-colored, long-sleeved shirts and pants reduce mosquito bites. Mosquitoes are attracted to dark colors and exposed skin.

Eliminate Standing Water

Sleep Under Mosquito Nets
Use mosquito nets if there's no air conditioning or screened windows. Especially important in high-risk areas like Jatrabari, Tejgaon, and Bangshal.

Secure Your Home
Install screens on windows and doors, keep doors closed, and repair any holes. This is one of the most effective prevention measures.

Treat Fever Promptly
Seek immediate medical attention if you experience high fever, severe muscle pain, vomiting, or rash. Early detection reduces complications.

Stay Hydrated
Drink plenty of water, oral rehydration solutions, coconut water, and fresh fruit juices to restore fluids and electrolytes lost during illness.

Eat a Balanced Diet
Prioritize vitamin C (citrus, papaya), iron, potassium, and protein-rich foods (eggs, lean meats, leafy greens) to rebuild blood cells and platelets.

Avoid strenuous exercise for the first few weeks. Begin with short walks or gentle stretching, and listen to your body's signals.
